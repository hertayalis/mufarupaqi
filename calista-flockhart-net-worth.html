What is Calista Flockhart’s Net Worth and Salary?

Calista Flockhart, the American actress, has a net worth of $30 million. She is well-known for her TV work in shows like “Ally McBeal” and “Brothers & Sisters.” Flockhart has also made appearances in movies such as “A Midsummer Night’s Dream” and “The Birdcage.”

During her career, Flockhart has won multiple awards, including Golden Globes and Screen Actors Guild Awards. She has also received three Primetime Emmy nominations.

Flockhart is married to the renowned actor Harrison Ford.

Early Life and Education

Calista Kay Flockhart was born on November 11th of 1964 in Freeport, Illinois. Her father’s job as a Kraft Foods executive meant that she spent her childhood in various locations across the country, including Illinois, Iowa, Minnesota, New Jersey, and New York. Her interest in the performing arts began at a young age when she wrote and performed her own play at a dinner party.

As a teenager, Flockhart attended high school in New Jersey before enrolling at the Mason Gross School of the Arts at Rutgers University. There, she received training in the Meisner technique under the tutelage of Bill Esper, a well-known acting teacher. Despite being new to the program, Flockhart was allowed to perform alongside older students and graduated in 1988 with a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in Theatre.

Calista Flockhart’s Acting Career

Calista Flockhart moved to New York after graduating from her theatre program and started attending auditions. She lived with a number of other women in a shared apartment, supporting herself with odd jobs. Her first major television role came not long after she had arrived in the Big Apple, and she booked a part in the series “Guiding Light.”

Flockhart also made it onto the stages of New York in a production of “Beside Herself.” Further TV roles came with series like “Naked in New York” and “Getting In.”

Her first speaking role in a film came with Robert Redford’s “Quiz Show.” In 1994, she experienced her Broadway debut with “The Glass Menagerie.” Calista won widespread critical praise for her performance, and she then joined the cast of the film “Drunks.”

After another appearance in “Jane Doe,” Calista booked a central role in the film “The Birdcage.” More Broadway roles followed, the most notable of which was probably a production of Chekhov’s “Three Sisters.”

In 1997, Calista’s career took a major step forward when she booked the starring role in the series “Ally McBeal.” She earned a Golden Globe for her performances in 1998, and the show was eventually canceled in 2002.

Flockhart then continued her television work with another role in “Brothers & Sisters,” which debuted in 2006. After the cancellation of “Brothers & Sisters” in 2011, Flockhart booked a role in the second season of “Full Circle” after a three-year acting hiatus. In 2015, she joined the cast of “Supergirl,” playing the character Cat Grant.

Outside of these notable roles, Flockhart has a number of other acting credits to her name. She starred as Helena in the film adaptation of Shakespeare’s “A Midsummer Night’s Dream” in 1999, appeared in “Things You Can Tell Just By Looking at Her” in 2000, and played Matthew Broderick’s deranged girlfriend in the 2004 film “The Last Shot.”

Calista Flockhart’s Relationship with Harrison Ford

In 2002, Calista Flockhart and Harrison Ford met at the Golden Globe Awards and began a relationship. Seven years later, the couple got engaged and tied the knot the following year. Prior to her relationship with Ford, Flockhart adopted a son in 2001. The couple are now the proud parents of this child.

Calista Flockhart’s Real Estate Ventures

Calista Flockhart and Harrison Ford purchased a traditional-style home in Los Angeles’ Brentwood neighborhood in 2011 for $12.65 million, which was 20% less than the original listed price. The property spans 13,767 square feet, featuring seven bedrooms, six fireplaces, and a detached cottage. The gated outdoor area includes a swimming pool, spa, and large lawn.

Prior to marrying Ford, Flockhart bought a 2,780-square-foot home in Brentwood for about $1.4 million, which she later put on the rental market for $10,000 per month. In 2012, Flockhart and Ford listed another Brentwood home for $8.295 million, which Harrison had owned since 1977, just after the release of the first “Star Wars” film.
