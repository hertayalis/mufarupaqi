Past Winners Of The Voice Where Are They Now?

Introduction to The Voice Winners

The Voice, NBC’s highly popular singing competition, has been a launchpad for numerous aspiring singers since its inception in 2011. With its unique format of blind auditions and celebrity coaching, the show has crowned many talented artists over the years. But what happens to these winners after their moment of victory? In this article, we delve into the post-show lives and careers of past winners of The Voice, exploring where they are now and how the show has impacted their musical journeys.

Javier Colon: The Inaugural Voice

Javier Colon, the very first winner of The Voice in 2011, set high expectations for future contestants. After his win, Colon released his album “Come Through For You” but eventually parted ways with Universal Republic Records. Despite the split, he continued to make music, releasing albums independently and performing across the country. His soulful voice remains a testament to the talent The Voice can discover.

Jermaine Paul: Season 2’s Soulful Champion

Jermaine Paul, previously a backup singer for Alicia Keys, took the crown in the second season. Post-Voice, Paul released a single “I Believe in This Life” and has been performing at various venues. He has also been working on his music, aiming to release more of his work to his fans.

Cassadee Pope: From Pop Punk to Country Stardom

Cassadee Pope, the winner of season 3, successfully transitioned from her pop-punk roots to a solo country artist. Her debut country album, “Frame by Frame,” topped the charts, and she has since been nominated for a Grammy. Pope continues to tour and release new music, solidifying her place in the country music scene.

Danielle Bradbery: The Young Country Phenom

At just 16, Danielle Bradbery won season 4 and quickly became a rising star in country music. She released her self-titled debut album and has since put out multiple records, including “I Don’t Believe We’ve Met,” showcasing her growth as an artist. Bradbery remains active in Nashville, collaborating with other artists and performing regularly.

Tessanne Chin: The Voice’s Reggae Fusion Artist

Tessanne Chin, the season 5 winner, brought a unique reggae fusion style to the show. After her win, she released her album “Count on My Love” and has been involved in various music projects. Chin continues to be a prominent figure in the reggae scene and performs internationally.

Josh Kaufman: A Diverse Musical Talent

Season 6 winner Josh Kaufman showcased his versatile voice on the show. Post-Voice, he starred in the Broadway production of “Pippin” and continues to perform live. Kaufman also teaches music and works on his own projects, demonstrating his passion for all aspects of music.

Craig Wayne Boyd: The Country Rocker

Craig Wayne Boyd won season 7 with his country-rock flair. He released his album “Top Shelf” and continues to tour, sharing his music with fans across the nation. Boyd’s dedication to his craft keeps him relevant in the country music industry.

Sawyer Fredericks: The Folk Artist with a Timeless Voice

At 16, Sawyer Fredericks’ distinct folk style won him season 8. He has since released several albums, including “A Good Storm,” and remains active in the folk music community. Fredericks tours extensively and has a dedicated fan base that appreciates his authentic sound.

Jordan Smith: A Voice of Power and Range

Jordan Smith’s powerful performances won him season 9. He released his debut album “Something Beautiful” and followed up with “Only Love” in 2018. Smith has also contributed to film soundtracks and continues to perform, showcasing his incredible vocal range.

Alisan Porter: From Child Star to The Voice Victor

Alisan Porter, known for her childhood role in “Curly Sue,” won season 10. She has since focused on her music career, releasing the album “Pink Cloud” and performing live. Porter’s journey shows her versatility and dedication to her artistry.

Sundance Head: The Soulful Country Artist

Season 11’s winner Sundance Head brought a soulful edge to country music. He released his album “Stained Glass and Neon,” collaborating with other country artists. Head continues to tour and share his music with a growing fan base.

Chris Blue: R&B Sensation

Chris Blue, the winner of season 12, captivated audiences with his R&B style. He has released singles like “Blue Blood Blues” and is working on more music. Blue’s performances and recordings continue to resonate with fans of soulful R&B.

Chloe Kohanski: Rock’s Modern Voice

Chloe Kohanski, now known as Chloe MK, won season 13 with her distinctive rock voice. She has since been exploring her sound, releasing alternative pop music and performing under her new moniker. Kohanski’s evolution as an artist keeps her fans excited for what’s next.

Brynn Cartelli: The Youngest Winner’s Journey

At 15, Brynn Cartelli became the youngest winner in season 14. She released her single “Last Night’s Mascara” and is working on her debut album. Cartelli’s youth and talent promise a long and successful career ahead.

Chevel Shepherd: Country’s Young Star

Chevel Shepherd, the season 15 winner, has stayed true to her country roots. She released the EP “Everybody’s Got a Story” and continues to perform. Shepherd’s dedication to country music has her poised for a lasting career.

Jake Hoot: The Country Crooner

Jake Hoot’s heartfelt country performances won him season 17. He released his EP “Love Out of Time” and continues to make music and perform. Hoot’s authentic approach to country music endears him to fans.

Todd Tilghman: The Soulful Pastor

Todd Tilghman, the season 18 winner, has been balancing music with his pastoral duties. He released his single “In a Heartache” and is working on furthering his music career while serving his community.

Carter Rubin: The Voice’s Young Prodigy

Carter Rubin won season 19 at just 15 years old, showcasing his pop potential. He is currently working on his music and navigating the industry as one of the youngest winners of The Voice.

Cam Anthony: A New Era of Talent

Cam Anthony, the winner of season 20, brought a fresh take to the show with his versatile performances. He is currently working on new music and has the potential to redefine genres with his unique style.

Girl Named Tom: Harmonizing Siblings

Girl Named Tom, the sibling trio that won season 21, captivated audiences with their harmonies. They are touring and working on original music, aiming to leave a lasting impact on the folk-pop scene.

Bryce Leatherwood: The Latest Voice Champion

Bryce Leatherwood is the most recent winner from season 22. As he embarks on his post-Voice career, fans are eager to see how he will use this platform to advance in the country music industry.

FAQs About The Voice Winners

  • How successful are The Voice winners post-show?

    Success varies among The Voice winners. Some have achieved significant success in the music industry, while others have found more modest careers. Factors such as genre, marketability, and individual drive play a role in their post-show trajectories.

  • Do The Voice winners get a record deal?

    Winners of The Voice typically receive a record deal as part of their prize. However, the longevity and success of that deal can vary, and some artists choose to pursue independent paths.

  • Has any winner of The Voice become a superstar?

    While many winners have enjoyed successful careers, none have reached the superstar status of some other reality show contestants like those from American Idol. However, artists like Cassadee Pope and Danielle Bradbery have made significant inroads in the country music scene.

Conclusion

The Voice has been a platform for many talented singers, providing them with exposure and opportunities in the music industry. While not all winners achieve superstardom, many have gone on to have successful careers, releasing albums, touring, and even venturing into acting and Broadway. The show’s impact on their lives is undeniable, and as each season brings new talent, the list of artists making their mark post-The Voice continues to grow.
