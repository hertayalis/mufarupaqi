Early Life and Career Beginnings

Christopher Russo was born in Syosset, New York, and developed a love for sports at a young age. He began his broadcasting career in the 1980s, working for various local radio stations before landing a job at WFAN in New York City.

Partnership with Mike Francesa

In 1989, Russo was paired with Mike Francesa to host the afternoon drive show, “Mike and the Mad Dog.” The show quickly became popular due to their lively debates and in-depth sports knowledge. Their partnership lasted for nearly 20 years, significantly boosting Russo’s profile and income.

Move to SiriusXM

In 2008, Russo signed a five-year contract with SiriusXM to host his own channel, Mad Dog Sports Radio. The move was a significant risk but ultimately proved successful, further increasing Russo’s net worth.

Current Contract and Salary

Real Estate Investments
