Lara Parker, whose real name is Mary Lamar Rickey, was an American actress known for her role as Angelique on the ABC-TV serial Dark Shadows, which aired from 1966 to 1971.

Lara Parker biography

Parker was born on October 27, 1938, in Knoxville, Tennessee, and spent her formative years in Memphis. She hailed from a prominent Southern family and was a descendant of Lucius Quintus Cincinnatus Lamar II, as well as Augustus Baldwin Longstreet, who was the uncle of Confederate General James Longstreet.

Parker commenced her undergraduate studies in philosophy at Vassar College before earning her Bachelor of Arts degree from Rhodes College (formerly known as Southwestern). She later pursued a Master of Arts degree in speech and drama from the University of Iowa.

Lara Parker career

Parker portrayed the character of “Laura Banner” in the opening sequence of the pilot episode of the television series The Incredible Hulk (1977).

Additionally, she played the role of “Madelaine,” a fashion model-witch, in the episode “The Trevi Collection” of Kolchak: The Night Stalker. Her extensive television credits include appearances on various shows such as Emergency (“One of Those Days” – Season 5, Episode 8), Kung Fu, The Six Million Dollar Man (“The Secret of Bigfoot Part 1” – Season 3, Episode 17), Police Woman, Kojak, Alice, Quincy, M.E., Hawaii Five-O, The Rockford Files, Highway to Heaven, Switch, Baretta, Galactica 1980 (“The Night The Cylons Landed”, Parts I & II), Barnaby Jones (“The Price of Anger” – 1980), the CBS daytime serial Capitol, and the ABC daytime serial One Life to Live.

Furthermore, she portrayed the character of Wanda, a secretary, in the 1977 television miniseries Washington: Behind Closed Doors, and had a recurring role in the short-lived television series Jessica Novak. Parker reprised her role as Angelique in Night of Dark Shadows, the second feature film based on Dark Shadows. She was joined by her fellow Dark Shadows cast members Kate Jackson, David Selby, Grayson Hall, Nancy Barrett, John Karlen, and Thayer David. This film deviated more loosely from the original series compared to House of Dark Shadows, and unfortunately did not achieve the same level of success at the box office.
