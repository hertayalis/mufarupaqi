Biography of Novak Djokovic

At the age of four, Djokovic started playing tennis after receiving a mini-racket and a soft foam ball from his parents, which according to his father, became “the most adored toy in his life.”

20 Grand Slam men’s singles titles, including a record nine Australian Open crowns, have been won by Djokovic.

Djokovic has amassed a total of 87 ATP singles championships, including a record 38 Masters events.

Djokovic is the first guy in the Open Era to earn a double lifetime Grand Slam and the only man to complete a non-calendar year Grand Slam.

In addition, Djokovic is the only player to have twice completed the ATP Tour’s Golden Masters.

The Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP) now has Djokovic ranked as the top player in the world, and he has held that position for a record-breaking 372 consecutive weeks.
